PROJECTS


1. Deep Learning Applications For The Detection Of Leaf Diseases Using CNN

Abstract:

            Agriculture is a critical sector, especially in countries like India where a large portion of the population depends on farming. However, plant diseases pose significant threats to crop yield and quality, leading to substantial economic losses. Traditional methods of disease detection, which rely on visual observation by farmers and experts, are often time-consuming, expensive, and prone to inaccuracies. In response, this project proposes an automatic plant disease recognition model based on Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). By utilizing advanced image processing techniques, the model classifies leaf images to accurately differentiate between healthy and diseased plants. Leveraging a custom dataset of diverse plant leaves, this CNN-based system significantly improves the speed and precision of disease detection. Early detection of plant diseases not only enhances agricultural productivity but also contributes to the sustainability of food supply and the economy. This project underscores the potential of computer vision and CNN applications in precision agriculture, offering a modern and efficient alternative to traditional plant disease diagnosis.

2. Designing Predictive Models For Automatic Disease

Abstract:

            Diabetes sickness upsurges the quantity of glucose in the blood triggered by a deficiency of insulin. Diabetes affects retina, heart, nerves and kidney. One important complication is DR. The mechanized methods for DR recognition are flexible for cost and time reduction and are more competent over manual analysis. Deep Learning technique performs computer aided medical diagnosis. This paper is an attempt toward finding an automatic solution for Diabetic Retinopathy disease in initial stage. Using Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning, doctors can find blind- ness before it happens. In this project we are using supervised learning approach to perform classification on fundus images. For this task we are employing several image processing procedures and filters to improve many significant features like microaneurysm, hemorrhages, exudates, swollen blood vessels which are the features of fundus image that imply that particular person has Diabetic Retinopathy and then using neural networks for classification. This classifies the fundus images with an accuracy of 82 percent by using CNN architecture.
